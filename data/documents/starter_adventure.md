# The Forgotten Crypt
*A D&D 5e Adventure for 3-5 Characters of 1st Level*

## Adventure Overview

The village of Greenhollow has been plagued by strange disappearances. Three villagers have vanished in the past week, all near the old ruins at the edge of the Whispering Woods. The local priest discovered an ancient crypt entrance beneath the ruins, sealed for centuries but now mysteriously opened. The adventurers are hired to investigate the crypt and rescue any survivors.

**Adventure Hooks**:
- The innkeeper's daughter, Elara, went missing yesterday. He offers 50 gold pieces for her safe return.
- A traveling merchant was attacked near the ruins. He escaped but his two guards vanished. He offers 100 gold and trade discounts.
- The village priest suspects undead activity and asks the heroes to cleanse the crypt for the good of the community.

## Part 1: The Crypt Entrance

### Scene 1: The Ruined Chapel
*Outdoor, daytime*

The adventurers arrive at the ruins of an ancient chapel, now overgrown with vines and moss. Stone pillars lie toppled, and a gaping hole in the floor reveals stone steps descending into darkness.

**What the Players See**:
- Weathered stone walls covered in ivy
- A broken altar dedicated to a forgotten god
- Fresh tracks leading down the stairs (goblins)
- Disturbed earth around the entrance

**Investigation (DC 12)**: The tracks belong to small humanoids, likely goblins, made within the past day.

**Perception (DC 14)**: Faint sounds of movement and whispered voices echo from below.

### Scene 2: Descending the Stairs
The stone stairs descend 30 feet underground. The air grows cold and damp. At the bottom, a stone corridor extends into darkness.

**Trap - Loose Step** (15 feet down):
- **Perception DC 13** to spot the loose stone
- Stepping on it triggers a collapse
- **Dexterity Save DC 12** or fall 15 feet, taking 1d6 bludgeoning damage and landing prone
- Can be disarmed with thieves' tools (DC 10)

## Part 2: The Crypt Levels

### Room 1: The Entry Hall
*30 feet by 20 feet, stone walls, dusty floor*

**Description**:
An ancient burial chamber with alcoves carved into the walls. Three stone sarcophagi line the center of the room. Torches flicker on the walls (recently lit by goblins). Two doorways lead deeper: north and east.

**Enemies**:
- **2 Goblins** standing guard
- They attack immediately but flee if one is killed

**Treasure**:
- In the north alcove: 15 silver pieces, a rusted dagger
- In the eastern sarcophagus: A potion of healing (2d4+2 HP)

**Perception (DC 12)**: Fresh footprints lead east.

### Room 2: The Spider's Lair (East Door)
*Circular room, 25 feet diameter, ceiling 20 feet high*

**Description**:
Thick webs cover the ceiling and corners of this round chamber. Ancient bones litter the floor. A large web stretches across the north section of the room.

**Enemies**:
- **1 Giant Spider** lurking in the webs above
- Drops down when party enters the center of the room
- Uses Web attack first, then Bite

**Trap - Web**:
- Characters walking through webbing must make **Strength Save DC 12** or be restrained
- Breaking free requires action and **Strength check DC 12**

**Treasure**:
- Wrapped in webbing: backpack with 20 gold pieces, silk rope (50 ft), and a silver holy symbol (25 gp)
- Hidden in bones: A +1 dagger with spider motif

### Room 3: The Goblin Den (North Door from Room 1)
*L-shaped room, 30 feet by 25 feet*

**Description**:
This chamber has been converted into a crude living space. Bedrolls, stolen supplies, and gnawed bones are scattered about. The smell is terrible. Elara, the innkeeper's daughter, is tied up in the corner, unconscious but alive.

**Enemies**:
- **3 Goblins** arguing over treasure
- **1 Wolf** chained near Elara as a guard
- Goblins fight desperately to protect their loot

**Tactics**:
- Goblins use Nimble Escape to hide behind debris
- Wolf is released on round 2
- One goblin tries to flee through the secret door to warn the boss

**Treasure**:
- Stolen goods: 45 gold pieces, 120 silver pieces
- A fine cloak worth 25 gold
- Three days of rations
- Elara's pendant (return for reward)

**Secret Door**:
- **Perception DC 14** to find
- Opens to passage leading to Room 5

### Room 4: The Collapsed Chamber (East from Room 2)
*Partially collapsed, 20 feet by 15 feet*

**Description**:
The ceiling has partially collapsed here. Rubble blocks most of the room, but a narrow gap leads to a small alcove.

**Hazard**:
- Walking across rubble requires **Dexterity Save DC 10** or make noise
- Noise alerts enemies in Room 5

**Treasure**:
- In the alcove: An ancient tome on burial rites (50 gp to a sage)
- A skeleton wearing a gold ring (15 gp)

### Room 5: The Ritual Chamber (Final Room)
*Large rectangular room, 40 feet by 30 feet, 15-foot ceiling*

**Description**:
A grand burial chamber with four stone pillars supporting the vaulted ceiling. An ornate sarcophagus dominates the center, its lid pushed aside. Dark runes glow faintly on the walls. Two villagers lie unconscious near the north wall. A makeshift altar made of bones sits in the corner.

**Enemies - Boss Fight**:
- **1 Orc Necromancer** (use Orc stats but add *Animate Dead* ability)
- **2 Skeletons** rising from the ground
- **1 Zombie** shambling from behind the sarcophagus

**Special - Necromancer Abilities**:
- **Necrotic Bolt**: Ranged spell attack +4 to hit, range 60 ft., 1d8 + 2 necrotic damage
- **Animate Dead** (1/day): Can raise 1 skeleton as a bonus action if there are bones nearby

**Tactics**:
- Skeletons engage in melee
- Zombie protects the necromancer
- Orc casts Necrotic Bolt from range
- If reduced below 5 HP, the orc tries to flee through secret tunnel

**Treasure**:
- On the altar: Necrotic ritual dagger (cursed, -1 to hit but +1d4 necrotic damage)
- In the sarcophagus: 150 gold pieces, a silver crown (75 gp), scroll of *Cure Wounds*
- Necromancer's pouch: 40 gold pieces, spell component pouch, amulet of protection (+1 to AC while worn)

**Rescuing the Villagers**:
- Both villagers are alive but poisoned
- *Cure Wounds* or *Lesser Restoration* removes poison
- Otherwise they recover naturally after 24 hours

## Conclusion

### Victory Rewards
- **Innkeeper's Reward**: 50 gold pieces for Elara's return + free room and board for a month
- **Merchant's Reward**: 100 gold pieces for information about his guards (they're the two poisoned villagers) + 10% discount on all goods
- **Priest's Blessing**: Blessing of protection (advantage on next death saving throw) + 25 gold donation from the church
- **Village Gratitude**: The party is celebrated as heroes, gaining free meals and drinks for a week

### Experience Points
- Defeating all enemies: 1,050 XP (divide among party)
- Rescuing all three villagers: 300 XP bonus
- Discovering the secret door: 100 XP
- Total Possible XP: 1,450 XP (enough for 3-4 characters to reach level 2)

## DM Notes

### Scaling for Party Size
- **2-3 Players**: Remove 1 goblin from each group, remove the zombie from final fight
- **5-6 Players**: Add 1 skeleton to final fight, increase orc HP to 20

### Optional Encounters
- **Wandering Skeletons**: If players make excessive noise, 1d2 skeletons investigate
- **Poison Gas Trap**: In Room 4, ancient mechanism releases gas (Constitution Save DC 12 or take 1d6 poison damage)

### Roleplaying Elara
- Age 17, frightened but brave
- Knows that the goblins were working for "the dark one" (the orc)
- Heard the orc talking about "raising an army"
- Can guide players to Room 5 if they rescue her first
